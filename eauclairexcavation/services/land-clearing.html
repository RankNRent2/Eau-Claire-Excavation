Land Clearing in Eau Claire, WI

Tree removal, brush clearing, stump grinding, and lot clearing for homeowners, developers, and rural landowners throughout Chippewa County and the Eau Claire area.

Turn Overgrown Land Into Usable Property

The Chippewa Valley has no shortage of wooded and brush-covered lots waiting to be developed — rural parcels, lakefront properties, wooded subdivisions, and overgrown agricultural land. Land clearing is often the first step toward building a home, expanding a farm, or simply getting your land usable again.

Our land clearing services include:

  • Tree removal — felling and removing trees of any size from your lot
  • Brush clearing — removing shrubs, saplings, and dense undergrowth by machine
  • Stump grinding — grinding tree stumps to 6–12 inches below grade so you can build or plant over them
  • Root removal — excavating large root systems that would interfere with construction or underground utilities
  • Debris hauling — chipping, burning (where permitted), or hauling cleared material off site
  • Selective clearing — removing only designated trees while preserving the ones you want to keep
  • Lot clearing for new construction — clearing the build envelope while leaving buffer trees in place

Residential Lot Clearing in Eau Claire

Buying a wooded lot and want to clear it for a new home? We work with homeowners throughout Eau Claire, Altoona, Fall Creek, Elk Mound, and beyond to open up build areas while respecting setbacks, shoreline rules (for lake and river properties), and your own preferences for shade and privacy.

Rural & Agricultural Land Clearing

We clear land for farmers expanding tillable acreage, rural landowners developing home sites, and recreational land owners opening fields for hunting, ATV trails, or food plots. We understand Chippewa County's DNR regulations around clearing near waterways and wetlands.

How Much Does Land Clearing Cost in Eau Claire?

Land clearing in the Eau Claire area typically runs $1,200 to $5,000 per acre, depending on tree size and density, stump count, slope, access, and what happens to the debris. Lightly wooded lots with small trees cost less; heavily wooded parcels with large hardwoods cost more. We'll walk your land and give you a written estimate at no charge.
