Utility Trenching in Eau Claire, WI

Precision trenching for water lines, sewer, electrical conduit, and fiber throughout Eau Claire and Chippewa County. Wisconsin Diggers Hotline compliant on every job.

Residential & Commercial Utility Trenching

Getting utilities from the street or well to your structure — or running new lines between buildings — requires a clean, correctly-sized trench dug to the right depth for each utility type. Wisconsin frost depths (up to 60 inches in the Chippewa Valley) dictate how deep water lines need to go. We know the local requirements and dig accordingly.

Utilities We Trench For

  • Water service lines — running from municipal water main or well to your home or business, installed below Wisconsin's frost depth
  • Sewer lines — connecting to municipal sewer or running to septic system components, with correct fall/slope per code
  • Electrical conduit — underground runs for service entrances, outbuildings, lighting, and equipment
  • Natural gas lines — working with your gas utility for underground service line installation
  • Fiber and low-voltage — cable TV, internet, phone, and security system conduit runs
  • Irrigation lines — trenching for residential and commercial irrigation systems
  • Septic system components — trenching for drain fields, distribution boxes, and tank access

Wisconsin Diggers Hotline — Required Before Every Dig

Wisconsin law requires a Diggers Hotline (811) call at least 3 business days before any excavation. This ensures existing underground utilities are marked before we dig. We handle the Diggers Hotline notification on your behalf as a standard part of every trenching project — you don't need to do anything.

Trench Depth Requirements in Eau Claire

The Chippewa Valley's frost depth means water lines typically need to be buried at least 60 inches deep to prevent freezing. Sewer lines are sloped at 1/4 inch per foot minimum toward the main. Electrical conduit depth depends on voltage and conduit type. We follow Wisconsin PSC and NEC requirements on every project.

Utility Trenching Cost in Eau Claire

Trenching costs depend primarily on length, depth, soil conditions, and access. A typical residential water service line run (80–150 feet) in Eau Claire costs $1,500–$4,000 including trench, backfill, and compaction. Longer commercial runs or deep sewer installations cost more.
