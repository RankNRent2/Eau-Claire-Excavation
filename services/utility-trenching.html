Utility Trenching in Eau Claire, WI

Precision trenching for water lines, sewer, electrical conduit, and fiber throughout Eau Claire and Chippewa County. Wisconsin Diggers Hotline compliant on every job.

Water lines, sewer connections, electrical conduit, and gas lines all need a properly dug and graded trench to be installed safely and to code. Utility trenching is precise work — get the depth, slope, or bedding wrong, and it can mean costly repairs long after the ground is filled back in.

Utilities Commonly Trenched For

  • Water service lines — running from municipal water main or well to a home or business, installed below Wisconsin's frost depth
  • Sewer lines — connecting to municipal sewer or running to septic system components, with correct fall/slope per code
  • Electrical conduit — underground runs for service entrances, outbuildings, lighting, and equipment
  • Natural gas lines — coordinated with the gas utility for underground service line installation
  • Fiber and low-voltage — cable TV, internet, phone, and security system conduit runs
  • Irrigation lines — trenching for residential and commercial irrigation systems
  • Septic system components — trenching for drain fields, distribution boxes, and tank access

What Utility Trenching Involves

  • Locating existing utilities — confirming what's already buried nearby before digging begins
  • Trenching to depth and grade — digging to the specific depth and slope required for the utility being installed
  • Bedding preparation — placing sand or gravel bedding so pipes and lines are properly supported
  • Backfilling and compaction — filling the trench in layers and compacting each one to prevent future settling
  • Surface restoration — regrading, and reseeding or repaving the surface once work is complete

Wisconsin Diggers Hotline — Required Before Every Dig

Wisconsin law requires a Diggers Hotline (811) call at least 3 business days before any excavation, so existing underground utilities can be marked before digging begins. This is arranged as a standard part of every trenching project.

Why Proper Trenching Matters Long-Term

A trench that's backfilled without proper compaction often settles unevenly over months or years, creating a visible dip in a yard or driveway — or worse, shifting the pipe itself out of grade. In this region's clay-heavy soil, poor compaction is a common cause of these delayed settling problems, since clay holds moisture and shifts more than sandy soil does when disturbed. The Chippewa Valley's frost depth (up to roughly 60 inches) also determines how deep water lines need to be buried to avoid freezing.

What Affects the Cost and Timeline

  • Depth required — sewer lines typically run deeper than water or electrical lines
  • Length of the trench
  • Soil type — rocky or clay-heavy ground takes longer to dig and backfill properly
  • Surface restoration needs — trenching under a driveway, lawn, or landscaping affects both cost and cleanup
  • Existing utility conflicts — nearby buried lines may require hand-digging in certain sections

Because utility depth requirements and soil conditions vary by property and by utility type, the most accurate quote comes from a site assessment rather than a flat per-foot estimate.

What to Expect During the Project

Most residential utility trenching projects take one to a few days, depending on length and depth. A Wisconsin Digger's Hotline call is required before any digging begins to confirm the location of existing utility lines nearby — this is a standard, non-negotiable step for safety.
