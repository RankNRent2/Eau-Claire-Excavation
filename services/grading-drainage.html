Grading & Drainage in Eau Claire, WI

Lot grading, French drain installation, swales, and erosion control to keep water moving away from your home and your property staying dry throughout Wisconsin's wet seasons.

Water problems on a property rarely start where they show up. A wet basement, a soggy yard, or standing water near a driveway is usually the result of how the ground is graded — and fixing it means reshaping the land so water moves away from the house instead of toward it.

What Grading and Drainage Work Involves

  • Site grading — reshaping the slope of the land so water flows away from structures
  • Swales and drainage channels — shallow, graded paths that redirect surface water
  • French drains — underground perforated pipe systems that collect and reroute water
  • Catch basins — collection points that gather water before it's piped away
  • Soil compaction — ensuring regraded areas are stable and won't settle unevenly over time

Why This Matters in Eau Claire and Chippewa County

The clay-heavy soil common throughout this region drains slowly compared to sandy soil, which means water tends to sit rather than absorb. Combined with Wisconsin's freeze-thaw cycles — where ground repeatedly freezes and thaws through the winter and early spring — poor grading can get worse year over year rather than staying the same, as settling and erosion compound each season.

Homes built on a slope, or lots situated below a neighboring property, are especially prone to water running toward the foundation unless the grading is specifically designed to redirect it.

What Affects the Cost and Timeline

  • Size of the area needing regrading
  • Severity of the existing problem — a minor slope correction is a smaller job than a full French drain installation
  • Soil type — clay-heavy soil sometimes needs additional drainage material worked in
  • Access — tight urban lots take longer than open rural properties
  • Existing landscaping or hardscaping that needs to be worked around or temporarily removed

Because drainage problems are often about the pattern of water flow across an entire property rather than a single spot, the most useful quotes come from a contractor walking the site, ideally after a rain if possible, to see exactly where water is collecting.

What to Expect During the Project

Most residential grading and drainage projects take a few days to about a week, depending on scope. Projects are generally easier to schedule in spring through fall, since regrading frozen ground is difficult and results are harder to verify until the soil thaws.
